Jose Rizal's Family Tree

The Rizals is considered one of the biggest families during their time. Domingo Lam-co, the family’s paternal ascendant was a full-blooded Chinese who came to the Philippines from Amoy, China in the closing years of the 17th century and married a Chinese half-breed by the name of Ines de la Rosa.

Researchers revealed that the Mercado-Rizal family had also traces of Japanese, Spanish, Malay and Even Negrito blood aside from Chinese.

Family Members

Francisco Mercado(Father)

José Rizal's father was a prominent landowner and a successful farmer. Father of Jose Rizal who was the youngest of 13 offsprings of Juan and Cirila Mercado. Born in Biñan, Laguna on April 18, 1818. studied in San Jose College, Manila. Died in Manila.

Teodora Alonso Realonda (Mother)

Teodora was an educated and influential figure in Rizal's life. she is the Mother of Jose Rizal who was the second child of Lorenzo Alonso and Brijida de Quintos. She studied at the Colegio de Santa Rosa. She was a business-minded woman, courteous, religious, hard-working and well-read. She was born in Santa Cruz, Manila on November 14, 1827 and died in 1913 in Manila.

Siblings

José Rizal had 11 siblings, who played significant roles in his upbringing. He has nine sisters and one brother.

Saturnina Rizal (1850-1913)

Eldest child of the Rizal-Alonzo marriage. Married Manuel Timoteo Hidalgo of Tanauan, Batangason on 3 November 1881, in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines. They were the parents of at least 3 sons and 2 daughters. She died on 14 September 1913

Paciano Rizal "lolo ciano" (1851-1930)

Only brother of Jose Rizal and the second child. Studied at San Jose College in Manila, became a farmer and a businessman. He had at least 1 son and 2 daughters with Severina Decena. He died on 13 April 1930. He served as Rizal's guardian while the latter was studying in Spain. Paciano was a huge influence to Rizal's life. He would constantly send money to Spain and update his younger brother of the friars' abuses in the Philippines through letters.

Narcisa Rizal (1852-1939)

Third child and second daughter. Married Antonio Lopez of Morong, Rizal on 6 June 1875. They were the parents of at least 5 sons and 4 daughters. She died on 24 June 1939

Olympia Rizal (1855-1887)

Fourth child and third daughter. Married Silvestre Ubaldo of Tanauan, Batangas about 1882. They were the parents of at least 3 sons. She died on 22 September 1887, in her hometown

Lucia Rizal (1857-1919)

Fifth child and fourth daughter. Married Mariano Herbosa of Calamba, Laguna on 27 February 1876. They were the parents of at least 4 sons and 7 daughters. She died on 25 December 1919, in her hometown

Maria Rizal (1859-1945)

Sixth child and fifth daughter. Married Daniel Faustino Cruz of Biñan, Laguna on 5 June 1886. It was to her whom Jose talked about wanting to marry Josephine Bracken when the majority of the Rizal family was apparently not amenable to the idea. Jose had also brought up to Maria his plan of establishing a Filipino colony in North British Borneo.

Jose Rizal (1861-1896)

Seventh child and only son. National hero of the Philippines. Known for his political writings that sparked the Philippine Revolution and finally resulted in his execution by the Spanish invaders, he was a versatile philosopher and political activist.

Concepcion Rizal "concha" (1862-1865)

Eighth child and sixth daughter. She passed away at three years old. It's said that Pepe was most in love with little Concha, who was a year younger than him.

Josefa Rizal (1865-1945)

Ninth child and seventh daughter. one of the original 29 women admitted to the Katipunan along with Gregoria de Jesus, wife of Andres Bonifacio Trinidad Rizal, historically, she became the custodian of Rizal's last and greatest poem.

Trinidad Rizal (1868-1951)

Tenth child and eighth daughter. was a Filipina feminist leader and co-founder of the Philippines' first feminist organization, the Asociación Femenista Filipina. Physician and writer, Dr. José Rizal.

Soledad Rizal (1870-1929)

Eleventh child and ninth daughter. Married Pantaleon Quintero of Calamba, Laguna on 23 June 1890. They were the parents of at least 4 sons and 2 daughters. She died on 26 August 1929
